Concept
Download your most revealing, personal moments. Share them with a machine. Now: remove yourself from the picture. outside of your body, your narrative now stands as text. the machine explains your story.

‘me.txt’ is one year of journal content, consumed and visualized by an algorithm. revealing pieces of information — thoughts, emotion, and repeated phrases — are highlighted. Uncategorized content is displayed raw - in chunks of black text.

Highlighted portions in this piece offer room to explore. Clicking on a highlighted element will display all other instances of that phrase within text, along with surrounding context. Picking apart these phrases: a story begins to emerge. Meaning is found in coherence, which rests on repetition on categorization.

In between highlighted text lies unique, uncategorized phrases. While these plain black strings form noise between categorized blocks, they also carry deeply personal and honest experiences.
Commentary
This project is of one two pieces exploring mediated self-expression. The other — 'Self-Portrait' — similarly displays segments of my journal. In ‘Self-Portrait’, raw information is also shared, but it is displayed within a curated context. The project is a self-portrait, in which thoughts and visual form are self-categorized.

Juxtaposed, these two pieces attempt to approximate the process of constructing narratives. Are we the most honest tellers of our own story? How important is coherence & logical consistency in understanding a person?
Technology
'me.txt' has two main components: backend server (written in node.js), and client side code for visualization. The server side piece parses text using natural language processing from the compromise.js library. The frontend matches input text to parsed phrases, and constructs visual elements with the help of d3.js. See full code here.
