Organization: Arcadia.io
Role: UX design, UX research, visual design, proof of concept development, strategy, implementation
Title: Product Manager, Care Product Line
Advisor: Nick Stepro, SVP of Product Strategy

Context

American healthcare incentivizes procedures: sicker patient populations often result in higher revenue for hospitals. In my day job, I work with organizations that are shifting to value-based care models that instead compensate for positive patient outcomes.

Value-based care necessitates holistic (addressing social needs) and preventive care. 'Care Management' programs are aimed at filling the gaps in care that a doctor does not typically provide: coordinating services, addressing social needs, and ensuring engagement with doctor recommendations. Typically, social workers and nurses work to fill these needs.

For the past two years (2017 onwards), I have been leading product development for Arcadia's care management suite. The product enables clinicians to better address the social needs of patients, and execute care at scale. I designed the (UX, visual, and product), reporting model, and workflow automation engine. I've also managed its implementation - both externally with care teams, and internally with the engineers executing on development. The product continues to scale, and is now used by more eight large healthcare organizations (with patient populations that range from 50,000 to 1 million).
