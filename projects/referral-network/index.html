Referral Network Explorer

Organization: Independent, data from Arcadia.io
Role: Design & development
Tags: data visualization
Tech: d3.js

Context

While developing a product for managing provider referrals, I became deeply curious in the broader landscape of referrals. So I visualized it.

For the past two years (2017 onwards), I have been leading product development for Arcadia's care management suite. The product enables clinicians to provide multi-faceted care - addressing medical, social, and housing needs.

I designed the interface (UX, visual, and product), reporting model, and workflow automation engine. I've also managed its implementation - both externally with care teams, and internally with the engineers executing on development. The product continues to scale, and is now used by more eight large healthcare organizations (with patient populations that range from 100,000 to 1 million).

Process

Building this project started first with the database. Within a SQL database at Arcadia.io, I combed through a list of all outpatient procedures. In order to assess cost variance in referrals, I had to constrain the data at a grain in which cost is consistent -- that is, the procedure (CPT) code level. I chose to focus on the most common procedure within each procedure category (i.e. 'Cardiovascular', 'Nervous'. After cleansing, categorized, and deidentifying the data, I took to javascript to visualize the dataset.
